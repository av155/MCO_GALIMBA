TINIKLING DANCE

What is Tinikling?

Tinikling is one of the oldest and most iconic traditional Filipino dances. Originating from the Visayas, it showcases the agility and grace of dancers as they skillfully step in and out of two bamboo poles that are rhythmically tapped and slid on the ground. The dance imitates the swift movements of the tikling bird as it avoids traps set by farmers. Tinikling is often performed during cultural fiestas, school programs, and national celebrations, representing Filipino creativity, rhythm, and resilience.

Key Characteristics of Tinikling

✔ Bamboo Pole Dance

Dancers step quickly between sliding bamboo poles, requiring timing and coordination.

✔ Fast and Energetic

Tinikling features lively footwork, rhythmic beats, and graceful movements.

✔ Cultural Symbol

Known as the national dance of the Philippines, representing tradition and artistry.
